WidgetMachine a new chance.

Wednesday, June 15, 2005 21:32  
As of Wednesday 15 June widget developers get a chance to get paid for some of the work they do.

When Apple first launched Dashboard many of us knew from the beginning what would happen because Konfabulator had done the exact same thing years earlier. People loved it, all sorts of free widgets where made for them to use. The down side was that more the 80% of the widgets available had either the same functionality or where just very poorly designed (technically aswell as visually).

With Apple basing Dashboard on web standards made creating widgets for this new platform far more easy as much more people now know how to use css, javascript and html then people then knew how to use xml and javascript for Konfabulator.

But the main problem remains, a very large percentage of widget created either has the same functionality or is just very poorly designed (technically aswell as visually). Why you might ask? Well mainly because making a widget that is really usefull and good looking takes a lot of time.

Not a lot of people realize it but when you want something to bring new functionality to either a widget or an application it takes time to create. You just can't base it on existing stuff as you want something new, so you really have to invest to make it from the ground up. But because in the past all widgets where free a problem arose.

Sure better widgets are possible, but it takes time and you need people with skills to make them. Not many skilled people do things for free. All of this causes the level of quality to stay relatively low compared to what could be possible. The only really good widgets now a days are created by companies making widgets that support and use their existing features of their applications or websites. But in general that doesn't result in inovative and new projects.

So that is where WidgetMachine comes into the story. If one looks at software development you see that the majority of applications available are shareware, meaning that the creator made it for limited free use and if one wishes to continue using it after a certain period of use they need to pay.

So when looking at widgets a logical step forward would be to create a platform where widget developers could freely create new and inovative widgets knowing they would get some sort of support, in this case money. They could use their own systems, but providing a service like Phillip Ryu created with WidgetMachine defenitly makes it easier for them.
