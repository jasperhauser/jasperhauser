State of Affairs

Tuesday, July 25, 2006 09:45  
I haven't had much time in the last weeks to write any new posts dues to the enormous amount of people that came over for work, and the enormous amount of work that resulted from that.

First Austin and Brain where here for two weeks, and Austin will return in September to stay here for another three months to further work on upcomming projects. We have a post almost ready about that little trip. Meanwhile I'm neck deep in another software project with local friends Koen and Dirk. Then the Cultured Code guys (Jurgen and Oliver) decided to come over to also start working on a new project I promised to work on, we worked a full week on that setting up the basic interface ideas. The mekentosj guys (Alexander and Tom) also came over for a quick meet and greet, perhaps we will do something together in the futured.


(Austin, Dominik, Brian, Martin, Koen, Jasper) A picture of our road trip visiting the CodingMonkeys in germany a couple of weeks ago.

The results of all the projects have been more than satisfying so far. But it's been a bit busy and I'm glad that I can go on holidays next week. It's been a "CocoaDevHouse" like no other, and I'm sure Blake would be proud :D. First I have to help out some developers who insist on releasing their apps round the Apple WWDC.

When I get back from holidays there are a bunch of project awaiting as-well. The project described above will all continue, I will also embark in a pretty big project with Phill Ryu. More on that later.

Yesterday Gil Turnbull from O'REILLY macdevcenter posted his story about what makes a good icon for which he did an interview with me and Jon Hicks a week ago. It's a bit short for such a relatively complex subject, but good non the less. Interestingly I have been working on several separate posts about icon design which I hope to finish and post during the holidays. Check back for that soon!
