This Mouse can bite.

Saturday, August 20, 2005 12:10  

2 days ago I got myself Apple's new Mighty Mouse. Before I bought it I plaid around with it for 5 minutes at my local Apple dealer. It felt really nice, light and natural to use. So I bought it. Now after use for two days I have my gripes about this mouse. Watch out this one can bite.

This seriously is the first mouse I ever had to "learn" to use. There are 2 things that can cause this little mouse to be very stubborn.

1) You have to place your fingers exactly on the two "button" sections beside the scroll wheel. If either of the two fingers is to close to the scroll wheel you will get erratic behavior. I constantly place my right hand index finger to close to the scroll wheel causing the mouse to interpret it as a right click. AAaaah this is annoying, I'll get used to it but I have to learn myself to place my fingers carefully.
2) When you grab the mouse to displace it on your table it often happens to me that I accidentally trigger the squeeze buttons, and whooosh I'm in Expose mode. Which also is pretty annoying.

So this morning I re-attached my old 4 button Logitech mouse again, only to realize how nice the Mighty Mouse actually is.

What I like most about the Mighty Mouse is that it's much lighter in weight, definitely lighter then my previous logitech mouse. The scroll wheel is a bliss, it's really light and easy to use. You go flying across pages in just the right speed and direction. The optical tracking is really good, no hick-ups there.

What I like less is the rather tough click, they could have made that slightly lighter. You really have to push. The one shell idea is great, but the finger location detection is causing some really annoying but "predictable" behavior causing me to relearn myself to carefully place my fingers on the mouse. Apple could have done a better job on this I think.

So my conclusion is that all in all it's a better mouse then the Logitech I had before, and it's light years past the old one button mouse Apple used to have. Apple did a great job. But I do hope they can fix the finger location detection to be better, perhaps with a software update. In general all my critique is cause by the fact that "I" am doing things the wrong way. But I'm not the only one.
